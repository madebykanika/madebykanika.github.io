Huddle Up

Huddle Up is a recreational sports app that tells the users' all about the venues and academies around - amenities, prices, slot timings, availability, coaches and book them easily from their mobile phones.

The Mission

Design a 1-stop platform that enables sports enthusiasts to view and book sports facilities, academies and playgrounds without any trouble.

My Role

As a brand, visual and experience designer, I was heavily involved in all phases of the project. I collaborated with Android developers to implement the design.

The Execution

The nature of the build would target Android users, early adopters, and a younger demographic. The ability to discover different sports being played in the users' vicinity and booking venues as per their convenience were the major features that were implemented and deployed in the first release.

The Result

We knew out of the gate that “viable” in MVP meant a stellar design, sound functionality, and an intuitive interface - all within a limited budget. The concept was developed - the user had the ability to view sports events that were happening in his locality and book them.


The concept promised to deliver a solid experience for sports enthusiasts who were unable to make sports a part of their routine - to book sports directly from their mobile device.

Problem and the Why

The storyboards helped me to further understand the user and problem space.
The storyboard below represents the journey of Gagan - a sports enthusiast who recently shifted to a new city and is interested playing a sport at the nearby sports facility.


“Gagan needs an easy way to discover sports venues near him and book them to avoid the hassle”


As you saw in the above story, there is a need for a solution for Gagan which can help him book and discover sports facilities around him. As a part of my exploration process, I discovered that this solution lacks the engagement component which makes it good for only intermittent use.

So, the overall goal of Huddle Up was to fill the gap and introduce a couple of things: Discoverability towards sports facilities, booking these facilities, and player engagement - with player profiles, tournament stats & scores and achievement gamification. I started with a vision of Huddle Up handling everything about bookings for the user.

Visual Design System

The essence of this app is user's enthusiasm and attachment to sports. In sports, a huddle is an action of a team gathering together, hence the name. The logo is inspired from "huddle".


The Solution

User Onboarding Flow

User Onboarding Experience is one of the most important aspect of this app as users'. Huddle Up was first of its kind product in the market, so it was essential to give these naive users' a good idea of what the app offers.

Different Sign In Options & Location Access

The app enables the users' to sign up using other platforms. After the user logs in, he is prompted to allow location access as it is one of the most important data that is required for a better user experience.

Let's book a tennis court

The idea was to design an intuitive booking process for the user. Since there are a number of selections that are required to be made before booking a sports venue, a well thought decision was taken to keep all the selections in a single page. Another challenge was not to overwhelm the user with multiple tasks, hence a gamified approach was adopted to make the user feel at ease with the product. This flow received positive feedback from the users during testing.

Easy Cancellations & Re-booking

User can easy manage his bookings. The app allows them to cancel and re-book venues in just one click

Usability Testing

The app was tested several times during the project

  • Lo-fi prototypes were tested with the team weekly to get feedback on the content, ease of use and interactivity of the app.
  • User testing - The users were asked to accomplish some hypothetical tasks using the app
  • Beta Testing - Before the release, we tested the app with the users' at several different sports facilities, cafes etc. They were using the app to book venues as part of the product marketing and promotions.

CONCLUSION

Next Steps

  • Although we had a solid MVP, I would still like to continue testing and improving the design.
  • Rolling out new features in future releases - Another problem that Huddle Up wanted to tackle was to increase engagement across the platform by introducing a tournament model, where in people can register their teams offline (as they prefer) and can track the scores, wins, losses etc through the app in real-time.

Reflections & Learnings

This project was challenging yet rewarding because I was able to work with the Huddle Up team to create the MVP for their product release. The product yielded successful task completions and positive feedback from the users.

  • Cross Team Communication - It is important to understand the language of the developers, stakeholders and project managers to accomodate UX needs.
  • Frequently Testing and Iterating - Keeping the stakeholders/users in loop and testing solutions as early as possible saves ample amount of time and re-work.
  • A plan for MVP - There were a number of fuzzy details that could lead to a number of design directions, so it is important to decide the scope of the design and create a plan for MVP.
