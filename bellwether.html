Bellwether

Bellwether is an effort to promote and sustain the rich heritage of Punjab in leather craft.

OVERVIEW

With the love for handicraft and passion for promoting the rich culture of Punjab, I cofounded this start-up in 2016. Bellwether is focused on designing footwear and accessories featuring designs inspired by rich culture of Punjab.

The idea was to support our artisans, create something that is unique and contemporary and take the quintessential Punjabi accessory - Punjabi Jutti to not only Punjabis but non-Punjabis as well.

My Role

Head of User Experience, Product and Strategy

Team

Navroop Dhillon, Pavleen

MOTIVATION

During my undergraduate education in Patiala, I spent my leisure time shopping and exploring the rich cultural heritage of the place. It was during that time I stumbled upon the Punjabi jutti - the most comfortable footwear that anyone can wear. I instantly fell in love with the craft. Further, during a conversation with a friend, we realized that not many people know about these hand-crafted jutties and it is time that we take a tiny part of our Punjabi tradition to places where it is not easy to source.
We decided to tread an unchartered path and ventured into the world of design and co-founded Bellwether.


What is Bellwether?

Bellwether is the brand name we envisaged owing to the product positioning we wanted to create in potential users minds. "Bellwether" is an indicator of trends. This being the first point of entry ‚Äì setting a trend of Jutties worldwide, we focused on the seller side of things, to get started on the first step to the product‚Äôs success.

Over the time, we worked to add a new-found sense of fun and a modern twist to the traditional juttis, making them just what you need to make your everyday adventures, extra-ordinary.

The decision of putting the products online, rather than putting them in the store, was a conscious one.

The only way you can capture buyers attention through clicks is by offering products, which you show and offer online. The idea was to take a tiny part of our Punjabi tradition to places where it is not easy to source. Now, people sitting in Mumbai or anywhere in can buy authentic Punjabi Juttis‚Äù.

User Interviews

I interviewed users who had experience shopping with Glossier or other beauty apps to understand the problem, identify pain points, and outline areas of opportunity. I was especially interested in investigating how people currently find their skincare products and what factors mattered most in choosing a qualified skincare product for themselves.

I found commonalities between users: the need for an personalised and unique shopping experience with a tool to help users get product recommendations tailored to their unique skin condition. These users expressed an especially difficult time shopping for skincare products than makeup products.

Competitive Analysis

I led a competitive analysis of existing products that uses modern technologies to provide skincare recommendation. The goal of this exercise was to identify competitor‚Äôs target user groups, product functionality, and usability to determine how Glossier could improve upon or avoid issues inherent to existing products. By identifying competitor strengths and weaknesses, I was able to better define Glossier‚Äôs value proposition. I reviewed the platforms such as Proven, HelloAva, BeautyByDesign, Revieve and Milk Makeup.

These companies solve this problem by incorporating AI driven skin quizzes, skin surveys, facial recognition technology and in their e-commerce apps.

NOVEL CONCEPT

Based on user research findings and competitive analysis, I decided to design a skincare product recommendation feature for Glossier. Glossier uses AI powered chatbot ‚ÄúGlossy‚Äù that offers a personal skin report and product recommendations to the user based on their unique skin type. I envision this product recommendation tool can effectively help users select the best matching skincare product with a smooth and simple process.

By providing a professional skin report and skincare product recommendations, Glossier empowers users to make decision with greater confidence and surity.

CONCLUSION

Next Steps

  • Although this project concluded with a solid prototype, I would still like to continue testing and improving the design. The next step would be to hand-off red lines and the final prototype screens to developers.
  • If I had more time for this project, the next step I would take is to consider how to implement this application with a data-driven approach.
  • Since a major component is the generation of personalized recommendations, I would want to consider ways to get the necessary user-data and provide a way for users to make note of what works for them and what does not.

Reflections & Learnings

Creating an end-to-end mobile app for a website was certainly a big and tricky project. I had to embody the existing experience, but improve at the same time. Matching the existing aesthetic while having freedom with visual elements was also challenging because I was free to run with ideas, but not deviate too much from the original aesthetic. I‚Äôm pleased with the end result which has hints of Glossier.
Another major learning was trust when it comes to solutions for problem spaces such as skin. Since skincare is a personal experience connected to one's health and wellness, it is important that the solution be reliable and trustworthy.
