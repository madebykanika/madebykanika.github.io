Zeit

Imagining a Time Travel tourism Website

OVERVIEW

For the first time in history, innovative technology will allow humans to travel back in time. Zeit‚Äôs new technology gives consumers the opportunity to have a travel experience that is not only unique, but out of this world--quite literally. With 289 destinations spanning prehistoric times to today, Zeit is offering time travel packages to many significant periods in history all across the world.

Client

Timeline

My Role

Design Tools

THE CHALLENGE

Create an e-commerce website that allows customers to to access information about time travel, browse through offered time/space locations, and book desired travel plans using a checkout feature within the website.

MY PROCESS AND WHAT I DID


DISCOVERY AND RESEARCH

Methodologies: Secondary Research & Primary Research (Questionnaire + User Interviews)

How to do research on something that doesn‚Äôt exist?

As there was no source available for time travel data, some of the research had to rely on secondary information and trends on regular travelling and travelers.

I began looking at INDIRECT competitors:

Zeit has no direct competitors that could have potentially given some insight into the working and functionality of a time travel product. I conducted a competitive analysis to identify competitors that offer unique experiences and looked at the approaches they follow to solve a similar problem.

The main insights from the competitive analysis were:

  • Users‚Äô rely on reviews and ratings from other travelers to plan their trips
  • Users‚Äô like to filter their trips based on time duration, price and ratings
  • Users‚Äô value the ability to add a trip to their wishlist
  • Users‚Äô want to see all the prices upfront

Primary Research

QUESTIONNAIRE

To understand people‚Äôs traveling habits and preferences, I distributed a survey and received 102 responses.



This data helped uncover users‚Äô traveling styles and views about time travel, that I will delve deeper into during the user interviews.

USER INTERVIEWS

I conducted four user interviews consisting of general questions about travel habits (how do you choose the destination, how do you book travel, obstacles with booking travels online) to eventually drilling down to specific questions regarding time travel.

KEY TAKEAWAYS

The biggest challenges faced
  • All the participants are passionate about traveling and usually plan their trips themselves as it gives them more flexibility
  • 2 out of 4 participants value experiencing something unique and special and gain new perspective.
  • All participants check reviews and recommendations before booking their trips.
  • Factors influencing their destination selection include affordability, amenities, trip duration and activities.
  • Participants conduct detailed research during their trip planning process which is frustrating for them.
  • Complicated booking sites, hidden fees and lack of detailed itineraries are the obstacles faced by participants in their current booking experience.
  • While they found time travel interesting and exciting, they were concerned about their safety, guarantee of return and messing the time stream.

DEFINE

Methodologies: Persona, Empathy Map, Storyboard & Feature Roadmap

Persona: Understanding the Users

By mapping out the significant characteristics I found from each of my user interview participants, two personas were created: Nick - the travel enthusiast, Rob - the historian

Their pain points in regard to time travel was the same - safety concerns, messing up the time stream, danger - but their needs and behaviors were quite different.

I focused on Rob as my primary persona to be targeted and Nick as a secondary persona. While some of the features might appeal to Nick as well, his needs and preferences could be more specifically targeted in future releases.

It was interesting to test the low fidelity prototypes. These were plugged into InVision as a prototype, which was then tested by three participants. They were asked to check what Zeit offers, find a trip to Rome and book it successfully. The data were recorded.

TEST

Methodologies: Usability Testing & Affinity Diagram

Before testing my prototype, I developed a usability test plan which included the project details, tasks and scenarios. I conducted two sets of 4 usability tests to collect user feedback on the website, the first to iterate upon my design, and the second to fine tune my designs. Participants were observed to see how they interact with the website, and to identify potential pain points while they are carrying out the task given at hand.
